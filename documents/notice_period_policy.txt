NOTICE PERIOD POLICY
====================

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Applicable To: All employees

1. STANDARD NOTICE PERIOD
   - Probationary employees (first 6 months): 2 weeks notice period.
   - Junior employees (0–2 years of service): 1 month notice period.
   - Mid-level employees (2–5 years of service): 2 months notice period.
   - Senior employees / Managers (5+ years of service): 3 months notice period.

2. NOTICE PERIOD DURING RESIGNATION
   - An employee wishing to resign must submit a formal written resignation letter to
     their reporting manager and HR.
   - The notice period begins from the date HR acknowledges the resignation.
   - The employee is expected to serve the full notice period unless a buyout is agreed upon.

3. NOTICE PERIOD BUYOUT
   - An employee may opt to buy out the remaining notice period by paying the equivalent
     salary for the unserved period.
   - The buyout amount must be approved by HR and the Finance department.
   - The company may also waive the notice period at its discretion.

4. NOTICE PERIOD DURING TERMINATION
   - In case of termination by the company (without cause), the employee will receive:
     - Equivalent pay in lieu of notice if the notice period is not served.
   - In case of termination for cause (e.g., misconduct, fraud), no notice or pay in lieu
     will be provided.

5. GARDEN LEAVE
   - During the notice period, the company reserves the right to place an employee on
     garden leave (paid, at home, non-working) while restricting access to systems.

6. HANDOVER OBLIGATIONS
   - The resigning/terminated employee must complete a formal knowledge transfer and
     handover of all ongoing responsibilities, documents, and access credentials.
   - Failure to complete handover may result in withholding of the final settlement.

7. FINAL SETTLEMENT
   - Full and final settlement (salary, reimbursements, leave encashment) will be
     processed within 30 working days of the last working day.
